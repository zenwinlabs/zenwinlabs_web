Privacy Policy

Short version: we don't know who you are.

Last updated: June 27, 2026

The short version

Habits does not collect your name, email, location, or any personal information. Your habit data lives entirely on your device and never leaves it.

The only data we collect is anonymous analytics events — things like which screens you visit and which features you use. Nothing that identifies you personally. Ever.

What we collect

✓ What we collect
  • Anonymous analytics events (screen views, feature usage, app open/close events)
  • Crash reports (anonymous, no personal data attached)
✗ What we never collect
  • Your name or email
  • Your location
  • Your habit data or entries
  • Any information that identifies you personally
  • Payment information

Your data stays on your device

Everything you track in Habits — your habits, streaks, history, reminders — is stored locally on your device using Android's built-in database. We have no access to it. We cannot see it. We cannot delete it remotely.

If you uninstall the app, your data is gone. We don't have a copy.

Analytics

We use anonymous analytics to understand how people use the app — which screens are visited most, which features are used, where people drop off. This helps us build a better product.

This data is fully anonymous. It cannot be traced back to you or your device.

Third parties

We do not sell your data. We do not share your data. We do not run ads.

The only third party involved is our anonymous analytics provider. They receive the same anonymous event data described above — nothing more.

Children

Habits does not knowingly collect any data from children under 13. Since we collect no personal data at all, this is straightforward.

Changes

If our privacy policy changes, we will update this page and the date at the top. Since we collect almost nothing, we don't expect this to change much.
